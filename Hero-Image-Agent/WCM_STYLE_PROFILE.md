# West Coast Modern — Visual Style & Taste Profile
## Extracted from westcoastmodern.ca + sold collection + email campaigns

_This document describes the WCM aesthetic in concrete, actionable terms for use in AI prompting, skill calibration, and photographer briefs._

---

## Brand Identity in One Sentence

West Coast Modern presents architect-designed homes as cultural objects — not commodities. The photography must feel like editorial coverage of a building, not a real estate listing.

---

## Visual Tone

**The WCM visual language sits at the intersection of:**
- Architectural photography (Dezeen, Wallpaper*, The Local Project)
- Pacific Northwest landscape photography (raw nature, dramatic light, coastal atmosphere)
- High-end real estate editorial (not MLS, not generic agent photography)

**Mood keywords:** calm authority, warm restraint, cinematic stillness, design confidence, aspirational without being showy

**Anti-keywords:** clinical, cold, transactional, over-saturated, HDR, fisheye distortion, stock-photo warm

---

## Colour & Light

### Preferred light conditions
- **Golden hour exterior** — warm, directional, long shadows across architectural planes; the house glows
- **Overcast diffused** — soft even light that shows material texture without harsh shadows; good for wood and concrete
- **Crisp clear morning** — blue-tinged, sharp edges, works well for glass and steel homes
- **Atmospheric dusk / lantern light** — deliberate, moody; the house is lit from within; sky is deep blue not black

### Colour palette that appears in WCM photography
- Warm natural wood tones (cedar, fir, teak)
- Raw concrete grey
- Forest green (trees, moss, landscape)
- Pacific blue-grey (ocean, overcast sky)
- Warm white (interior walls, snow-capped backgrounds)
- Black steel window frames as accent

### What to avoid
- Over-saturation or heavy HDR processing
- Cool blue-grey that reads cold rather than serene
- Flashgun-lit interiors that flatten depth
- Yellow interior tungsten light that clashes with exterior blue sky

## Composition Principles

### Exterior hero shots
- **Full architectural read** — the building's personality must be legible from a thumbnail
- **Sky or landscape as frame** — breathing room above and around the structure
- **Foreground ground plane** — driveway, garden, path, or natural ground plane anchors the shot
- **The 2/3–1/3 split** — house occupies roughly two-thirds of frame; sky or landscape one-third (or inverse when sky is the story)
- **Strong geometric line** — rooflines, cantilevers, or deck edges should create a leading line into the frame

### Interior shots
- **View through spaces** — shooting through doorways or corridors creates depth
- **Window as light source** — natural window light should be the dominant source; no blown-out windows
- **Furniture at an angle** — straight-on furniture shots feel flat; diagonal placement creates dimension
- **Scale reference** — include one element (chair, plant, lamp) that tells the viewer how large the space is

### Universal
- Horizon is level (unless deliberate and strong)
- Verticals are vertical (no converging lines unless controlled tilt-shift)
- Rule of thirds is a floor, not a ceiling — WCM compositions are often more deliberate

## Architecture Types in WCM Portfolio

The AI should recognise these typologies and score hero images accordingly:

| Type | Hero shot priorities |
|---|---|
| **Mid-century modern** | Show roofline geometry, wood cladding, low horizontal profile against landscape |
| **Contemporary glass + steel** | Show transparency, framing of view, interior/exterior relationship |
| **Brutalist concrete** | Show massing, shadow play, texture of raw concrete, dramatic angle |
| **West Coast timber frame** | Show warmth of wood, integration with forest or ocean setting |
| **Clifftop / waterfront** | Show site drama — the setting IS the story |
| **Infill urban** | Show the architectural confidence of a bold building in a dense context |

## What "West Coast" Means Visually

These elements signal WCM's geographic and cultural context:

- **Cedar and fir** siding, decking, screening — warm reddish-brown tones
- **Lush Douglas fir forest** as backdrop — deep saturated green
- **Pacific Ocean or Burrard Inlet** — steel blue, dramatic horizon
- **Mountain silhouette** — North Shore mountains as backdrop
- **Rain-soaked surfaces** — wet wood, wet concrete, puddles; can be beautiful and atmospheric
- **Arbutus trees** — distinctive twisted red bark; pure Pacific Northwest
- **Stone walls and moss** — natural integration of the built and natural

---

## What This Brand Is NOT

These are things that would actively hurt a WCM image:

- Suburban context (neighbouring houses, power lines, street furniture)
- Heavy landscaping that looks "manicured" rather than natural
- Cars in driveways
- Pool shots as the lead image (supporting, not hero)
- Symmetrical front-on shots of the facade — this is not a builder's spec sheet
- Shots that look like they could be any house anywhere

## Social Media Aesthetic (Instagram)

WCM's Instagram (`@westcoastmodernhomes`) is an editorial feed, not a listings feed:

- Images have **room to breathe** — not over-cropped to fill the frame
- A mix of **horizontal crops** (letterboxed in feed) and native verticals for stories
- Grid reads as a **cohesive editorial spread**, not a series of individual posts
- Captions are architectural and cultural, not promotional
- Video reels use ambient sound + music rather than voiceover

### For Instagram carousel covers:
- Vertical format (4:5 or 9:16 for Stories)
- Hero vertical shot leads
- Warm, slightly de-saturated grade (not punchy or over-contrasted)
- No text overlays on the image itself

## Email Visual Language (MailChimp)

Based on WCM email templates:

- **Hero image is full-width horizontal** — the email opens with a single powerful image
- Below: **vertical images in a grid** — 2 or 3 across, tight spacing
- **White background with minimal text** — the architecture speaks; copy is secondary
- **No borders or drop shadows** on images
- Overall email feels like a magazine preview, not a real estate mailer

## Photographer Brief Summary (One Page)

If you need to brief a photographer using this document:

> West Coast Modern photography should feel like it belongs in Wallpaper* or The Local Project — not on a standard real estate listing. We want the house to read as architecture, not as square footage. Golden hour or atmospheric overcast light. Full architectural read in the hero, with breathing room for sky and landscape. West Coast materials — cedar, concrete, glass — should be visible and textural. Clean foregrounds. No cars. No clutter. The hero shot should tell the story of why this particular house is remarkable. It should make someone stop scrolling.
